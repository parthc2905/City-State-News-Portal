Mumbai's New Metro Line Opens Next Month

The long-awaited Metro Line 3 connecting South Mumbai to the suburbs will begin operations on April 15, cutting average commute times by 40 minutes.

Rajesh Kumar
Published on March 10, 2026 at 10:30 AM

Mumbai residents will soon have a faster way to navigate the city's notorious traffic. The Mumbai Metro Rail Corporation (MMRC) announced today that Metro Line 3, also known as the Aqua Line, will begin commercial operations on April 15, 2026.

The 33.5-kilometer underground corridor will connect Cuffe Parade in South Mumbai to the Seepz business district in the northern suburbs, with 27 stations along the route. The project, which cost ₹23,136 crore, has been in development since 2016.

Transforming Daily Commutes

"This is a game-changer for Mumbai's public transportation," said Ashwini Bhide, Managing Director of MMRC. "Commuters who currently spend 90 minutes traveling from Cuffe Parade to SEEPZ will now complete the journey in just 50 minutes."

"I've been waiting for this for years. My daily commute from Worli to Andheri takes almost two hours during peak time. This metro line will cut that in half."
— Priya Desai, daily commuter

The metro will operate from 5:30 AM to 11:30 PM daily, with trains arriving every 3-4 minutes during peak hours and every 8-10 minutes during off-peak times. Each train can accommodate approximately 1,500 passengers.

Key Features and Stations

The Metro Line 3 includes several notable features:

  • Fully underground: India's first fully underground metro line
  • Modern trains: Air-conditioned coaches with digital displays and CCTV
  • Accessibility: All stations equipped with elevators and facilities for differently-abled passengers
  • Integration: Seamless connectivity with existing metro lines and suburban railway stations

Major stations along the route include Bandra-Kurla Complex, Dharavi, Dadar, Siddhivinayak Temple, and Churchgate. The BKC station, one of the largest on the network, will serve as a major interchange point.

Environmental Impact

According to MMRC estimates, the new line is expected to reduce vehicular traffic by approximately 350,000 trips per day, significantly lowering carbon emissions in the city. The metro will be powered by renewable energy, with solar panels installed at depot facilities.

"This project aligns with Mumbai's commitment to sustainable urban development," said Municipal Commissioner Iqbal Singh Chahal. "We expect a 15% reduction in traffic congestion in the affected corridors within the first year of operation."

Ticket Pricing and Passes

Fares will range from ₹10 to ₹50 depending on the distance traveled. Monthly and quarterly passes will be available at discounted rates. Students and senior citizens will receive a 50% discount on fares.

MMRC has also announced the launch of a mobile app for ticket booking and real-time train tracking, expected to go live on April 1, two weeks before the metro's inauguration.

Looking Ahead

With Line 3 nearing completion, Mumbai Metro is already planning expansions. Line 4 (Wadala-Kasarvadavali) and Line 5 (Thane-Kalyan) are currently under construction, with projected completion dates in 2027 and 2028 respectively.

The city aims to have a comprehensive metro network covering over 150 kilometers by 2030, transforming Mumbai into one of India's best-connected cities.
